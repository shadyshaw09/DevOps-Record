Day 01 - Introduction to DevOps
Date:10-02-2026
Objective: To understand the fundamentals of DevOps and its importance in modern software development.
Theory:
DevOps is a combination of two words: Development (Dev) and Operations (Ops).
It is a culture and practice that brings development and IT operations teams together to improve collaboration and productivity.

Traditionally, developers write code and operations teams deploy and maintain it. This separation often caused delays and conflicts. DevOps solves this problem by encouraging continuous communication, automation, and integration.



DevOps focuses on:

- Continuous Integration (CI)

- Continuous Delivery (CD)

- Automation

- Monitoring

- Collaboration



The main goal of DevOps is to deliver high-quality software faster and more reliably.


 DevOps Lifecycle:



1. Plan

2. Develop

3. Build

4. Test

5. Release

6. Deploy

7. Operate

8. Monitor



This lifecycle is continuous and iterative.

 Advantages of DevOps:



- Faster software delivery
- Reduced deployment failures
- Better collaboration
- Improved customer satisfaction
- Faster bug fixes

 Conclusion:



Today I learned the fundamentals of DevOps, its lifecycle, and how it improves collaboration between development and operations teams.
