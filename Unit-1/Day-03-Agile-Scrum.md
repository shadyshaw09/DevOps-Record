Day 03 - Agile and Scrum
Date: 12-02-2026

Objective:  To understand Agile methodology and the Scrum framework used in modern software development.
Theory:
Agile is a software development methodology that focuses on flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement. Unlike traditional models like Waterfall, Agile allows changes even during development.



Agile promotes:

- Customer collaboration

- Working software

- Responding to change

- Iterative development



Development is divided into small cycles called iterations.

Scrum Framework:
Scrum is the most popular Agile framework. It organizes work into fixed time periods called Sprints (usually 2–4 weeks).

Scrum Roles:



1. Product Owner  

- Defines product requirements  
- Manages product backlog  



2. Scrum Master  

- Facilitates Scrum process  
- Removes obstacles  



3. Development Team  
- Develops and tests the product  
crum Artifacts:



- Product Backlog

- Sprint Backlog

- Increment

Scrum Events:



- Sprint Planning

- Daily Standup

- Sprint Review

- Sprint Retrospective

Advantages of Agile:



- Faster feedback

- Improved collaboration

- Flexible to changes

- Continuous improvement

Conclusion: Today I learned about Agile methodology and the Scrum framework, including its roles, artifacts, and events.
